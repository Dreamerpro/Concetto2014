Minero_Uno

Introduction

Robotics has found its use in almost every field of Engineering, Mineral Processing is one of them. Mining & Mineral Processing Industry has seen exponential growth in recent times. To meet the high standards, the industry has to come up with new and better technology for both faster and reliable mineral processing. The use of robotics has given the industries an upper hand in coping up with the increasing needs. The basic 3 steps include - i) Exploration ii) Exploitation iii) Extraction This event mainly focuses to test the efficiency of participating robots in these tasks.

Abstract

General Rules:

1. Each team can have a maximum of 5 participants.
2. A team may consist of students from different colleges.
3. The event will be held in two rounds.
4. When a team is called for match, they must report within ten minutes
or else they might lose their chance.
5. Points will be deducted from the team, if the bot causes any damage to the arena.
6. No more than 2 electrical slots will be given.
7. The organizers reserve the right to change the rules as they seem fit.
8. Judges decision will be final and binding to all.

Bot Specification:

1. The bot size should be less than 40cm * 40cm * 60cm (length, breadth, height).
2. The bot can be of utmost 8 kg.
3. Teams have to show and declare all of their bots before their first match itself.
4. No major changes in the bot will allowed after the above mentioned declaration.
5. Drill mechanism may be added after completion of Round 1.
6. The bot will be disqualified in case it damages the arena.
7. The pushing arm can be made by using hydraulics, spring or motor etc.

Round 1:

Geo-Futsal


Problem Statement:
The bots will have to traverse rough terrain and score maximum possible goals within 5 minutes. There will be 7 minerals (in form of balls) in the arena namely Diamond, Sapphire, Topaz, Emerald, Amber, Jasper and Ruby in which putting Ruby into goal will need the bot to score one more goal within 30 seconds to get the points allotted to Ruby.

Rules:

1. The balls can only be pushed to goal.
2. No arm mechanism can be used in this level.Arena Specifications:
1. The arena will be in the form of a rectangle 3.5m X 3 m.
2. The arena will consist of several hurdles in the form of uneven surface, tremors, pebbles, obstructions etc.

Arena Specifications:

1. The arena will be in the form of a rectangle 3.5m X 3 m.
2. The arena will consist of several hurdles in the form of uneven surface, tremors, pebbles, obstructions etc.


Point System:


1. Points will be awarded for scoring goals.
2. Points will be awarded as:-

Diamond100

Overall points scored will be taken into account for preparing the rank list.
Top 10 teams will qualify for the 2nd round.


Round 2:
Problem Statement:
The bots will have to traverse on rough terrain and discover minerals. The minerals discovered need to be taken out from ground and then it must be sent to the processing plant. Then the bot has to drill holes in the walls that will be in the arena with in the allotted time. Finally, there will be wells where the bot is supposed to suck the fluid from it and pour it in the container. The allotted time for doing the above said is 3+2+1 minutes, the most versatile bot will win. General Rules:
1. The bot must have a proper picking, drilling and sucking mechanism.
2. Arm mechanism may be used for transporting the Mineral (block) to the processing zone.
3. Simple push mechanism for transportation will lead to deduction of points.
4. Drilling should have rotating mechanism.
5. Suction mechanism can be made from motors, syringes or pumps.

Arena Specification:


1. Dimensions: 4m x 3m
2. The arena will consist of rough terrain.
3. The bot will have to traverse through obstacles.
4. The processing zone will be shown in red colour.


Point System:
1. For drilling a certain zone, 20 points will be awarded.
2. For transporting a single block to the processing zone, 50 points will be awarded.
3. For sucking out oil from a well, 50 points will be awarded.
4. Points will be awarded according to the amount of minerals found inside the blocks.
1 unit of Mineral A=50 Points
1 unit of Mineral B=40 Points
1 unit of Mineral C=30 Points
1 unit of Mineral D=20 Points
1 unit of Mineral E=10 Points

For example, A team drills 4 zones, transports 2 blocks to the processing plant, sucks oil of 1 well, and collects 2 units of Mineral A, 3 units of Mineral B, 1 unit of Mineral C, 5 units of Mineral D and 1 unit of Mineral E then the total points earned by the team will be, =(20x4)+(50x2)+(50x1)+(50x2)+(40x3)+(30x1)+(20x5)+(10x1)
=80+100+50+100+120+30+100+10
=590
