Robowar

Introduction

This Robowar experience high adrenaline levels as fierce monstrous robots battle it out for supremacy. Expect intense competition as teams fight it out in the hell-cell and unleash their fury. Definitely not for the weak hearted!!

Abstract

2. PROBLEM STATEMENT

Design a wired/wireless, manually controlled machine that is capable of totally overpowering its opponent on the war-front and push it onto the various weapons which will be present on the arena.

3.0 GAME PROCEDURE

3.1 Each team can have maximum of five members.
3.2 Match duration: 4 minutes.
3.3 Winning criteria: Team can either win by direct knockout or if the time runs out and there's no knockout or both bots are inactive, the winner will be decided by the final points
3.4 A robot will be declared inactive if it cannot display any movement for a time period of 30 seconds.
3.5 First round (league match): First round would be a league match where teams will be divided in the groups of three . The team with highest points will go to the next round.
3.6 Knock out rounds : Further rounds will be knock out rounds where Teams will be divided in the group of two's and the winner of the match will go to the next round. The final match will be a knock out match .


4.0 BOT SPECIFICATIONS

4.1 The initial dimension of bot should not exceed 40cm x 40cm x 40cm. However there is no limitation on bot dimension once the match starts. Any machine component should not detached (intentionally) during any point of the war.
4.2 The weight of the machine should not exceed 20 kgs. (Excluding remote control and remote control wires). In case of a wireless bot weight will be counted as (0.8x actual weight).
4.3 In case of wired bots, the wires should remain slack at any instant during the fight. All the wires coming out of the machine should be stacked as a single unit.
4.4 In case of wireless system, it should have a minimum three frequency remote control circuit .
4.5 The machine can be powered electrically only.
4.6 The electrical voltage at any point of time in the machine should not exceed 40 V DC/AC.
4.7 In case of wireless bot batteries should be placed on the bot also teams cannot use 230V(AC) external supply.
4.8 In case of wired bots teams A/C will be provided.
4.9 Jumping and hopping is not allowed. Flying is not allowed.
4.10 Participants can use pneumatic and hydraulic weapon systems but use of external pressure/ liquid cylinders are not allowed.
4.11 Robots can have any kind of cutters, flippers, saws, hammers, lifting devices etc. as weapons, with the following exceptions:
Liquid projectiles ,Acid based Weapons ,EMP generators ,Any kind of flammable liquid ,Flame-producing weapons ,Any kind of explosive material ,Nets, glue.

5.0 Arena specifications:

5.1 Arena will be a hexagonal wooden plane. For more information check the figure.
5.2 5% error could be present in actual track.
4.2.2 Arena weapons: there are 3 pushers placed alternatively. they are manually controlled weapons. It can be used to push the opponent. They are centered along the side. It will have a dimension of 50cm x 40 cm x 5cm



6.0 SCORING

6.1 Points are earned by attacking opponent team with arena weapons. In case of no knock outs or if both bots are inactive, winner will be decided by points criteria.
6.2 Pushers: There are three pushers in the arena; each pusher is controlled by a team. Using pusher on opponent’s bot will earn you 10 points.
6.3 In case of pushers points will be awarded only if it has some considerable impact on the opponent’s bot and will be decided by the event coordinators.
6.4 Arena will have 3 pushers and 3 walls alternatively if someone gets hit to a wall he will get -10 and that particular wall will be blocked or closed until someone gets hit to another wall .
6.5 During the match,if the bot becomes inactive or for any other reason need external manual help 10 marks will be deducted from the concerned team

7.0 VIOLATIONS

7.1 If the bot operator is seen to be using the wire from the controller to pull, move or hold the bot, i.e. if the wire becomes taught at any point, the team will be disqualified.
7.2 All efforts must be made to protect battery terminals from a direct short and causing a battery fire, failure to do so will lead to direct disqualification.
8.0 SAFETY

8.1 Compliance with all event rules is mandatory. It is expected that competitors stay within the rules and procedures of their own accord and do not require constant policing.
8.2 Participants are expected to abide by the rules & should co-operate with the organizers.
8.3 If you have a robot or weapon design that does not fit within the categories set forth in these rules or is in some way ambiguous or borderline, please contact the event organizers.
