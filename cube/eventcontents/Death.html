Death Race

Introduction

It's a race for life, not between humans but their metallic counterparts. Ideal for those who want to see a bunch of machines competing against each other in extreme terrains.

Abstract


OBJECTIVE


The arena will consist of several challenging hurdles and the bots will have to make their way through in the least possible time.

TEAM SPECIFICATIONS


1. Participants of the same or different college can take part as a team.
2. Number of members in the team should be between 3 to 5.

BOT SPECIFICATIONS


* Bot must not exceed 250mm x 250mm x 200mm as l x b x h in dimensions.
* Weight of robot must not exceed 5 kg.



RULE BOOK


1. Damage to the arena will lead to disqualification.
2. Bots will strictly have to follow path restrictions.
3. There can only be one manual controller per round at a time.
4. Teams will be provided AC 220 Volts (Home Supply), but no adapter or regulator will be provided so team must bring their own devices.
5. If at any point controller is needed to touch the robot during the run (like if the bot falls or get struck somewhere), then the robot has to start over from the previous checkpoint.
6. The machine will be checked for its safety before the race and will be discarded if found unsafe for other participants and spectators.
7. Judges decision will be final and binding.

ARENA DESCRIPTION



1. Track is a dirt track.
2. There will be certain obstacles in the race track which will try to slow down the robot.
3. The track surface and coarse line may have unevenness.
4. There will be abrupt angles (sharp turns).
5. Arena consists of obstacles such as wedge, pebbles, sand, fire, water jet, bridges, speed brakes, one sided gates, narrow pathways etc.
6. Different combinations of the obstacles will be used during subsequent levels.

* Final path will be revealed at the time of the event.
* Arena will be opened 1 hour prior to the start of first round for teams to have a trial.
* The arena shown below is for indicative purpose only. The original arena may differ.




ROUNDS


* Event will be of 3 rounds which will organized on 2 Consecutive days.
* Difficulty level will increase with every round.


ELIMINATOR

* Conducted on Day 1
* This round will have least difficulty level.
* Two bots will run on track simultaneously and their respective timings will be noted.
* Based on their timing, Top 20 teams will progress to the next round.


CHALLENGER

* Conducted on Day 2
* This round will have increased level of difficulty.
* Number of obstacles & blockages will be more.
* Each team will have to complete 2 Laps.
* Top 4 teams (taking 4 least timings to complete race) will progress for subsequent round.


ULTIMATE

* Initial ranking of teams will be done on the timings of previous round.
* 1st & 3rd ranked teams will compete against each other.
* Subsequently, 2nd & 4th ranked teams will compete.
* Winners of the 2 duals will compete for the final glory.

* Any changes & updates regarding event schedules and rules will be notified on the website, teams are requested to keep themselves posted.

PENALTY

The robot is not allowed to go out of the track. If it goes then violation will be considered and accordingly points will be deducted as per judge's discretion.
After that, the teams have to start the game from the last checkpoint crossed. While doing so the participants will be allowed to put the robot by hands.
