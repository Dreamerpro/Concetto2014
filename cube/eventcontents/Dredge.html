Dredge and Drop

Introduction

Often during the cleaning process in ores, a lot of unwanted material like sand, stones and other non-biodegradable human waste etc. is mixed with minerals which need to be cleaned.
"Dredge and drop" basically gives the idea about separation, carriage and storage techniques employed in mining.

THEME:

Participants are required to design bots which need

* to transport a specific quantity of material from one place to another
* to separate unwanted material like over burden (sand, rocks) from ore (minerals)

Abstract


STAGES OF VALIDATION:


ROUND 1:

* In this round the bots have to simply transport sand from one place to another in a time span of 5 minutes.
* Ones’ which carry larger amount (among the appeared participants) are qualified for next round

ROUND 2:

* In this round, the bot shall have to travel across a slippery terrain and reach a destined position where there would be a closed pit, inside which, marbles and stones mixed with sand are buried.
* One’s bot had to uncover the sand and collect the marbles and stones.
* The bot that’s mined, marbles to stones ratio and in total number is more in a permitted time (of 7 min) is considered as winner.

BOT SPECIFICATIONS:

* Size and weight of the bot should have a certain limit (not more than 20x30cm2 and 3kg respectively).
* If a conveyer belt is used then the length and width should not exceed 25cm and 8 cm respectively. (conveyer belt will be helpful in collecting minerals faster and easily)
* If a collector jar or box is used, max permissible volume is 20x15x10cm3.

RULES:

* Pit dimensions are 1.5m diameter and 0.5m depth
* Only 2 bots would be competing against one other simultaneously.
* The bot should be able to run over the sand.
* Power is supplied by us.
* Bots that are bought directly from market are strictly prohibited
* The event organisers are not responsible for any type of electrical or mechanical damage to the bot.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

* Open to all, in a team of three to five participants
